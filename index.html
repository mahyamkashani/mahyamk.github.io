Intro

I’m Mahya Mohammadi Kashani (Mahya MK) and I am a PhD student in Computer Science at IT-University of Copenhagen by mid-August 2021 in Copenhagen, Denmark. I also am one of ESRs (Early Stage Researcher) in REMARO (Reliable AI for Marine Robotics) network .

My PhD project mainly concentrates on designing a Bayesian-inference-based prospection method to improve the reliability of underwater robots. I aim to develop a probabilistic decision-making model to assess risks and use this model to assist in plan selection. Before joining the REMARO (Reliable AI for Marine Robotic) group, my main research interests focused on pattern recognition algorithms, particularly sparse reconstruction/coding and search space reduction in the Automatic Image Annotation task.

Computer Vision Engineer

Jan 2020 - Current Robotic Lab- SRTTU

Projects: Vision of Waste Segregation Robot; Android Application to control/setup a new costumized Treadmill.

Activities: I work as a computer vision engineer in Robotic Lab under supervision Dr. Rahmani in Mechanical Engineering Department. I work on automatic waste resegregation. The goal of project, in my section, is about designing vision of robot to detect and track objects automatically such as PET (polyethylene terephthalate). Polyethylene terephthalate is known as PET, and it is the most widely recyclable plastic in the world. Generally, We aim to design and develop vision of the robot which segregate these kind of plastics as human done before.

Our Computer Vision team, including me,have customized, one of the most popular state-of-the-art Object Detection by transfer learning and also, use Object Tracking such as,Deep Sort, method to find PETs among wastes in first phase. Furthermore, We work on Camera Calibration, finding and mapping the object's center point in Live recording. We use various industrial cameras in order to do the project. It is worth noting that I work with two control/ electrical engineers to build our control system due to communication with robot's movement engines. In order to do this, data is transferred by serial ports.
